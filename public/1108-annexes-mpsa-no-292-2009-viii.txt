4.0 Project Area Description                                                                          <br /><br /><br />
4.1 Terrain/Physiography                                                                              <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                                      <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                                      <br /><br /><br />
                     The MPSA applied area is characterized by undulating to                          <br /><br /><br />
                     moderately sloping in a generally rolling terrain. Undulating land               <br /><br /><br />
                     form is concentrated at the valley floor while sloping form describe             <br /><br /><br />
                     the slope of hills. Ground elevation ranges from 10 meters to 200                <br /><br /><br />
                     meters above mean sea                   level (amsl).                            <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                                      <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                                      <br /><br /><br />
 4.2 Accessibility                                                                                    <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                                      <br /><br /><br />
 The applied area can be reached from Tacloban City by a three (3)-                                   <br /><br /><br />
 hour ride via the well-paved coastal highway passing thru Lawaan                                     <br /><br /><br />
 town to Guiuan. Thence, a two (2)-hour pump boat ride to the                                         <br /><br /><br />
 island.    -    ___________                                                                          <br /><br /><br />

﻿4.3                      Dr .linage System                                  <br /><br /><br />
                                                                            <br /><br /><br />
                                                                            <br /><br /><br />
The applied area is principally drained by the tributaries of the           <br /><br /><br />
Cantelado and Salog Rivers. These tributaries empty their load to           <br /><br /><br />
the Philippine Sea.                                                         <br /><br /><br />
4.4    Vegetation                                                           <br /><br /><br />
The vegetation covering the MPSA applied area generally consists            <br /><br /><br />
of shrubs and cogon grasses, and second growth trees.                       <br /><br /><br />
Most of the agricultural lands are dominantly planted with coconut          <br /><br /><br />
trees. Other major crops include vegetables, rootcrops, palay, corn,        <br /><br /><br />
banana and other fruit trees, coffee and pineapple.                         <br /><br /><br />
4.5    Land Use                                                             <br /><br /><br />
The Municipality of Guiuan has a total land area of 175.49 square           <br /><br /><br />
kilometers. It is composed of sixty (60) barangays and the only             <br /><br /><br />
town in the Province of Eastern Samar with biggest number of                <br /><br /><br />
island barangays. Existing land use indicates a predominance of             <br /><br /><br />
agriculture use which covers 38.2% of the total land area. Most of          <br /><br /><br />
the agricultural lands are dominantly planted with coconut trees.           <br /><br /><br />
The MPSA applied area is basically a mineral land, being                    <br /><br /><br />
previously covered by the various Mining Lease Contracts of                 <br /><br /><br />
Cambayas Mining Corporation, and mining operations of Heritage              <br /><br /><br />
Resources and Mining Corporation.                                           <br /><br /><br />

Description of Exploration Works                                            <br /><br /><br />
Base on the data from previous operations by Heritage Resources and         <br /><br /><br />
Mining Corporation, the chromite resource in the applied area is            <br /><br /><br />
conservatively estimated at around 40 million metric tons. Further study    <br /><br /><br />
shall be carried out to possibly delineate economic resource of chromite    <br /><br /><br />
﻿deposit. Exploration activities to be conducted are outlined and discussed <br /><br /><br />
below.                                                                     <br /><br /><br />
5.1 Research Work                                                          <br /><br /><br />
Pertinent and necessary research works will be carried out by the          <br /><br /><br />
project proponent. This work includes collation of geological data,        <br /><br /><br />
previous reports and geological maps that may be available in the          <br /><br /><br />
Mines and Geosciences Bureau and other private and/or                      <br /><br /><br />
government agencies concerned, which may have conducted                    <br /><br /><br />
studies in the subject MPSA applied area, particularly those that          <br /><br /><br />
focus in chromite and other associated minerals.                           <br /><br /><br />
5.1.1 Survey of Previous Works on the Area                                 <br /><br /><br />
5.1.1.1    Nature or Type of Study or Undertaking                          <br /><br /><br />
Gathering of information pertinent to the previous                         <br /><br /><br />
works undertaken in the MPSA applied area will                             <br /><br /><br />
focus on the geology and mineralization. The                               <br /><br /><br />
Mines and Geosciences Bureau published and                                 <br /><br /><br />
unpublished reports regarding mineral                                      <br /><br /><br />
occurrences and other potential economic mineral                           <br /><br /><br />
deposit in the MPSA applied area shall be                                  <br /><br /><br />
reviewed.                                                                  <br /><br /><br />
5.1.1.2    Duration                                                        <br /><br /><br />
The research work/data gathering activity is                               <br /><br /><br />
expected to be undertaken for about two (2)                                <br /><br /><br />
months. The budget for this activity is estimated                          <br /><br /><br />
at One Hundred Thousand Pesos                                              <br /><br /><br />
(PhP100,000.00), which includes the salary of the                          <br /><br /><br />
technical personnel and miscellaneous expenses                             <br /><br /><br />
(e.g.    transportation/communication/food                                 <br /><br /><br />
allowances, reproduction of pertinent data, etc.).                         <br /><br /><br />
This activity will commence once the MPSA                                  <br /><br /><br />
﻿5.1.1.3    Coverage                                  <br /><br /><br />
The whole MPSA applied area will be covered by       <br /><br /><br />
the research work/survey.                            <br /><br /><br />
5.1.1.4    Proponent                                 <br /><br /><br />
The proponent is the MPSA applicant itself thru its  <br /><br /><br />
technical personnel that shall conduct the           <br /><br /><br />
research work.                                       <br /><br /><br />
5.1.1.5    Results or Conclusion Arrived at          <br /><br /><br />
The result of the said research work, should it be   <br /><br /><br />
successful, will be very vital in the implementation <br /><br /><br />
of the exploration work program, which is            <br /><br /><br />
expected to lessen the works involve and thus        <br /><br /><br />
reduce total exploration costs.                      <br /><br /><br />
5.1.2 Data Compilation/Collation                     <br /><br /><br />
5.1.2.1    Geochemical/Geophysical Data              <br /><br /><br />
Available Geochemical and Geophysical data from      <br /><br /><br />
previous studies conducted in the area will be       <br /><br /><br />
considered to primarily locate potential chromite    <br /><br /><br />
prospect.                                            <br /><br /><br />
5.1.2.2    Lithological Data                         <br /><br /><br />
Lithological data shall be complied and collated     <br /><br /><br />
based on the findings on previous reports on the     <br /><br /><br />
area. Using the data, confirmatory study shall be    <br /><br /><br />
undertaken during the actual field study.            <br /><br /><br />
﻿ 5.1.2.3 Mineralization/Alteration Studies                      <br /><br /><br />
                                                                <br /><br /><br />
The target mineral deposits in the area is                      <br /><br /><br />
chromite, but other mineral deposits will also be               <br /><br /><br />
given emphasis. Interview with expert Geologists                <br /><br /><br />
who have already conducted various studies in the               <br /><br /><br />
MPSA applied area, and if possible, request                     <br /><br /><br />
copies of their reports to predetermine, among                  <br /><br /><br />
others, the mineralization in the area, including               <br /><br /><br />
their observations on the geological alteration.                <br /><br /><br />
5.1.2.4    Various Thematic Maps Covering the Target            <br /><br /><br />
Area                                                            <br /><br /><br />

complied and reproduced to serve as base map                    <br /><br /><br />
during the exploration period.                                  <br /><br /><br />

2.0 Type and Nature of Project                                                                                                <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                                                              <br /><br /><br />
The proposed project covering approximately Three Thousand Five                                                               <br /><br /><br />
Hundred Sixteen Point Two Thousand Fifteen (3,516.2015) hectares is                                                           <br /><br /><br />
primarily an exploration project geared toward the characterization and                                                       <br /><br /><br />
assessment of chromite and other minerals within the area applied for the                                                     <br /><br /><br />
Mineral Production Sharing Agreement. The proponent has an estimated                                                          <br /><br /><br />
budget of PhP1,871,000.00 for the first year and PhP5,947,000.00 for the                                                      <br /><br /><br />
second year implementation of the Exploration Work Program and 10% of                                                         <br /><br /><br />
the exploration cost to be allotted to the Environmental Work Program is                                                      <br /><br /><br />
Php782,000.00.                                                                                                                <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                                                              <br /><br /><br />
The main objective of the project is to pursue a sound and systematic                                                         <br /><br /><br />
exploration program to characterize and qualify the resource available in                                                     <br /><br /><br />
the area, to maintain a cost effective operation, to make certain the                                                         <br /><br /><br />
viability and sustainability of the project and to ensure that and                                                            <br /><br /><br />
environmentally friendly mining operation could put in place and will set a                                                   <br /><br /><br />
model for similar operations in the area.                                                                                     <br /><br /><br />

﻿4.0 Description of Existing Environment                                <br /><br /><br />
                                                                       <br /><br /><br />
 4.1    Land Environment                                               <br /><br /><br />
 4.1.1    Topography/Physiography                                      <br /><br /><br />
                                                                       <br /><br /><br />
 The MPSA applied area is characterized by undulating to               <br /><br /><br />
 moderately sloping in a generally rolling terrain. Undulating         <br /><br /><br />
 land form is concentrated at the valley floor while sloping           <br /><br /><br />
 form describe the slope of hills. Ground elevation ranges             <br /><br /><br />
 from 10 meters to 200 meters above mean sea level (amsl).             <br /><br /><br />
 4.1.2    Land Use                                                     <br /><br /><br />
                                                                       <br /><br /><br />
 The MPSA applied area is basically a mineral land, being              <br /><br /><br />
 previously covered by the various Mining Lease Contracts of           <br /><br /><br />
 Cambayas Mining Corporation, and mining operations of                 <br /><br /><br />
 Heritage Resources and Mining Corporation.                            <br /><br /><br />
 4.1.3    Pedology                                                     <br /><br /><br />
                                                                       <br /><br /><br />
 The data pertaining to this study can be gathered as part of the      <br /><br /><br />
 Exploration Work Program particularly in the excavation of test pits  <br /><br /><br />
 and/or trenches. Although the analyses and description of             <br /><br /><br />
 excavation and excavated materials focus on the economic              <br /><br /><br />
 minerals that may be present, most of the data recorded/gathered      <br /><br /><br />
 contain vital environmental information.                              <br /><br /><br />
 4.2    Water Environment                                              <br /><br /><br />
                                                                       <br /><br /><br />
 The principal drainage system that could affect the area are the      <br /><br /><br />
 tributaries of the Cantelado and Salog Rivers. Surface run-off of     <br /><br /><br />
 the two (2) river systems drained towards the Philippine Sea.         <br /><br /><br />
﻿ 4.2.1 Water Quality                                                 <br /><br /><br />
                                                                     <br /><br /><br />
Values of the other elements including the other parameters          <br /><br /><br />
such as total dissolve solids, total suspended soils, biological     <br /><br /><br />
oxygen demand and dissolve oxygen will be determined                 <br /><br /><br />
immediately as soon as the exploration activities commence.          <br /><br /><br />
Water samples from the affected water bodies will be                 <br /><br /><br />
collected quarterly, and will be sent to reputable and               <br /><br /><br />
Department of Environment and Natural Resources-                     <br /><br /><br />
accredited laboratory, for analyses. The results shall serve         <br /><br /><br />
as the baseline data for the proposed project.                       <br /><br /><br />
4.2.2 Hydrology                                                      <br /><br /><br />
The existing hydrological profile in the area can best be            <br /><br /><br />
interpreted once the geological work is completed. This will         <br /><br /><br />
be complemented by other hydrological works, such as,                <br /><br /><br />
measurements of recharge rate, water level in the existing           <br /><br /><br />
well, and other related work/activities.                             <br /><br /><br />
4.3    Climatology/Rainfall                                          <br /><br /><br />
The MPSA applied area and the vicinity belong to Type II of the      <br /><br /><br />
Climate Classifications in the Philippines. Hence, no dry season     <br /><br /><br />
with a very pronounced maximum rainfall from November to April       <br /><br /><br />
and wet for the rest of the year.                                    <br /><br /><br />
4.4    Geological/Geomorphological Environment                       <br /><br /><br />
With the exception of the Recent Reefal limestone skirting the       <br /><br /><br />
shorelines of the whole island, the MPSA applied area is generally   <br /><br /><br />
underlained by ultramafic rock complex made up of variable           <br /><br /><br />
proportions of harzburgite, wherlite and dunite that are usually     <br /><br /><br />
serpentinized.                                                       <br /><br /><br />
The ultramafic rocks are presumably part of the ophiolite mass       <br /><br /><br />
﻿trusted over into the southeastern and middle portion of Samar           <br /><br /><br />
Island. The ophiolite mass belongs to the Eastern Bicol-Eastern          <br /><br /><br />
Mindanao ophiolite belt, and are interconnected beneath the              <br /><br /><br />
intervening seas.                                                        <br /><br /><br />
The wherlite peridotite is grayish black when fresh and becoming         <br /><br /><br />
yellowish brown to rusty brown when weathered. It is medium to           <br /><br /><br />
coarse grained and is slightly serpentinized. Informal mineralogical     <br /><br /><br />
analysis conducted identified the constituent minerals as enstatite,     <br /><br /><br />
antigorite, magnetite, clinopyroxene and olivine. The harzburgite is     <br /><br /><br />
yellowish green when fresh and turns to brown when weathered.            <br /><br /><br />
The harzburgite generally have a zenoblastic granular texture and        <br /><br /><br />
consist of relict olivine, orthopyroxenes, chrysotile, magnetite and     <br /><br /><br />
chromite. The outcrop is intensely serpentinized. The dunite which       <br /><br /><br />
is the host rock of the chromite mineralization is generally greenish    <br /><br /><br />
black, fine grained and consisting dominantly of olivine.                <br /><br /><br />
Serpentinization is slight to pervasive.                                 <br /><br /><br />
4.5    Biological Environment                                            <br /><br /><br />
In general, the MPSA applied area is covered by natural vegetation       <br /><br /><br />
consisting of grass/shrub formations. Most of the agricultural lands     <br /><br /><br />
are dominantly planted with coconut trees. Other major crops             <br /><br /><br />
include vegetables, rootcrops, palay, corn, banana and other fruit       <br /><br /><br />
trees, coffee and pineapple.                                             <br /><br /><br />
4.6    Socio-Economic Environment                                        <br /><br /><br />
Fishing is the main source of livelihood for the residents living in the <br /><br /><br />
area and vicinity, the area being almost surrounded by bodies of         <br /><br /><br />
water. Agriculture/farming is the alternative source of livelihood.      <br /><br /><br />
Lifestyle is simple, typical of the rural area. Generally,               <br /><br /><br />
unemployment/lack of employment opportunities tops the list of the       <br /><br /><br />
major problems in the area. One of the primary factors is                <br /><br /><br />
inadequate educational attainment. Other factor is the scarcity of       <br /><br /><br />

﻿6.0 Identification of Potential Environmental Effects            <br /><br /><br />
                                                                 <br /><br /><br />
 6.1    On Land                                                  <br /><br /><br />
 6.1.1    Surface Disturbance Off the Mineral Property           <br /><br /><br />
                                                                 <br /><br /><br />
 There is no expected surface disturbance off the applied        <br /><br /><br />
 area considering access to and from the target site is readily  <br /><br /><br />
 available and that there is no potential natural or artificial  <br /><br /><br />
 obstruction that would hinder accessibility. Existing roads     <br /><br /><br />
 and access ways will be utilized and no activity will be        <br /><br /><br />
 undertaken outside the applied area.                            <br /><br /><br />
                                                                 <br /><br /><br />
 6.1.2    Surface Disturbance on the Mineral Property            <br /><br /><br />

 excavation of test pits and trenches. Some of the               <br /><br /><br />
 present vegetative cover, which are mostly shrubs,              <br /><br /><br />
 bushes and grasses may be uprooted/removed                      <br /><br /><br />
 from the surface.                                               <br /><br /><br />
 6.1.2.2    Change in Rate of Erosion                            <br /><br /><br />
 Removal of existing vegetation as a consequence                 <br /><br /><br />
 of test pits/trenches excavations and drilling may              <br /><br /><br />
 increase the rate of erosion.                                   <br /><br /><br />

7.0 Environmental Management Measures and Total Costs                  <br /><br /><br />
7.1 Progressive Rehabilitation/Restoration of Excavated Areas          <br /><br /><br />
Areas affected by excavations during test pitting, trenching and       <br /><br /><br />
drilling shall be progressively rehabilitated and/or restored through  <br /><br /><br />
backfilling with top soil. Backfilled areas will be replanted with     <br /><br /><br />
suitable grass or tree species to prevent erosion and to restore the   <br /><br /><br />
vegetative cover in the area.                                          <br /><br /><br />
﻿7.2    Management of Stockpile of Excavated and Removed Earth          <br /><br /><br />
Excess excavated materials will be brought to a pre-designated         <br /><br /><br />
stockpile area where it will be safely confined without causing        <br /><br /><br />
siltation to the surrounding environment.                              <br /><br /><br />
7.3    Maintenance of Roads                                            <br /><br /><br />
Existing access roads will be utilized and maintained. Dusts           <br /><br /><br />
accumulation will be properly controlled by periodic spraying with     <br /><br /><br />
water. Proper drainage systems shall be maintained to prevent          <br /><br /><br />
and/or minimize soil erosion due to water inundation.                  <br /><br /><br />
7.4    Handling of Toxic and Hazardous Materials                       <br /><br /><br />
Solid and liquid wastes generated during the conduct of the            <br /><br /><br />
exploration works shall be disposed of properly to prevent possible    <br /><br /><br />
contamination of the surrounding.                                      <br /><br /><br />
7.5    Accommodation of other Economic Activities                      <br /><br /><br />
Other economic activities, such as farming, fishing and other          <br /><br /><br />
agricultural activities will not be prevented during the course of the <br /><br /><br />
exploration.                                                           <br /><br /><br />
7.6    Alternative plans if Special Habits of Flora and Fauna are      <br /><br /><br />
Affected                                                               <br /><br /><br />
Although no special habits of flora and fauna are expected to be       <br /><br /><br />
affected by the exploration activities, possible areas will be avoided <br /><br /><br />
as much as possible and preservation plans and measures will be        <br /><br /><br />
formulated and implemented.                                            <br /><br /><br />
﻿7.7 Socio-economic Mitigating Measures                                 <br /><br /><br />
                                                                       <br /><br /><br />

Dialogue between the Company and Population                            <br /><br /><br />

7.7.2    Working Environment and Protection Measures for               <br /><br /><br />

Safety of workers/laborers will be treated with utmost                 <br /><br /><br />

After the exploration activities have been completed and prior to      <br /><br /><br />
