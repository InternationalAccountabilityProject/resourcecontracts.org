The exploration areas are generally characterized by moderate to            <br /><br /><br />

4.1.2    Land Use/Capability                                                <br /><br /><br />
The proposed areas are classified as mineral lands. The surrounding         <br /><br /><br />
areas are suited for grazing, hence the presence of many cattle ranches.    <br /><br /><br />
Although an estimated 1/10 of its total land area is flat and suitable for  <br /><br /><br />
agricultural/vegetative crops, the residents prefer the quick and more      <br /><br /><br />
lucrative gold panning as their means of livelihood.                        <br /><br /><br />
Makeshifts houses made of nipa, bamboo and light wooden materials           <br /><br /><br />
are found along areas where slopes are more favorable and closer to         <br /><br /><br />
the access road.                                                            <br /><br /><br />
4.1.3    Pedology                                                           <br /><br /><br />
The topographical relief features distinct vegetation growth, reflective    <br /><br /><br />
of the dominant soil type found in the applied area. The mountainous        <br /><br /><br />
eastern and southern sections of the project area have shallow soil         <br /><br /><br />
accumulations (clays and talus), mainly due to moderate and steeply         <br /><br /><br />
sloping ground.                                                             <br /><br /><br />
The eastern mountain areas generally represent the mineralized ground       <br /><br /><br />
where most of the exploration activities will be conducted. Poor            <br /><br /><br />
vegetation growth at this section is largely due to thin and mineralized    <br /><br /><br />
soil cover. Slight to moderate erosion are confined on rill and gullies.    <br /><br /><br />
The unmineralized western part of the applied area are characterized        <br /><br /><br />
by flat to very gently sloping grounds, mostly grasslands and patchy        <br /><br /><br />
ricefields in alluvium soil.                                                <br /><br /><br />

replacement within the fracture and about 1-3 meters beyond,              <br /><br /><br />
argilization (bleaching) is most intense. Further away, chloritization    <br /><br /><br />
and pyritization predominates and the gold and silver values die out.     <br /><br /><br />
Ore values are only within the quartz veins and the argillized zones.     <br /><br /><br />
Calcite dilutes the gold concentration and chalcopyrite is occasionally   <br /><br /><br />
 seen.                                                                    <br /><br /><br />

﻿6.0 IDENTIFICATION OF POTENTIAF ENVIRONMENT EFFECTS                                <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                   <br /><br /><br />
 6.1    On Land                                                                    <br /><br /><br />
 With the road network already existing during the operation of Atlas              <br /><br /><br />
 Consolidated Mining and Development Corporation (AC’MDC). there is no             <br /><br /><br />
 significant potential environmental effects identified on constructing access     <br /><br /><br />
 roads. The planned exploration activities will only involve the construction of   <br /><br /><br />
 short and narrow feeder roads.                                                    <br /><br /><br />
 The possible environmental effect may come during movement of drilling            <br /><br /><br />
 equipment and drillsite preparations. The drilling operations will strip rock     <br /><br /><br />
 materials and the vegetation growth within the confines of the drilling site.     <br /><br /><br />
 Drilling operations will produce some noise and fumes/dust. There will be         <br /><br /><br />
 minimal surface disturbance and soil contamination considering that existing      <br /><br /><br />
 roads and short extensions will be utilized for access to the drill sites.        <br /><br /><br />
 The exploration campsites will be properly maintained with proper sanitation      <br /><br /><br />
 to allow full reclamation. Non-biodegradeable waste (e.g. container drums,        <br /><br /><br />
 lube oils, grease and other exploration waste will be removed from the            <br /><br /><br />
 exploration site and disposed of properly. The exploration team will be           <br /><br /><br />
 quartered at the existing mine facilities since the housing compound is close to  <br /><br /><br />
 the exploration sites.                                                            <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                   <br /><br /><br />
 6.2    Changes in hydrolog}                                                       <br /><br /><br />
 Major flooding will be a remote possibility due to a limited watershed and        <br /><br /><br />
 drainage network. There will be no major ecological alteration. Vegetation        <br /><br /><br />
 that will be destroyed will be replenished or compensated by the present          <br /><br /><br />
 reforestation/afforestation project of the company. Existing hydrologic cycle     <br /><br /><br />
 will thus be maintained. Water used in drilling is returned uncontaminated.       <br /><br /><br />
