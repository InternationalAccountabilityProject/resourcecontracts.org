      incorporate the material as it may be from time to time amended. If the<br /><br />
      material is repealed, the reference is to the material that replaces the repealed<br /><br />
      material, as determined by the CONTRACTING OFFICER.<br /><br />
 (iv) There are no oral provisions to this Contract, the whole agreement is in<br /><br />
      writing. At the time of signing, this document reflects the whole agreement<br /><br />
  (v) Future modifications or additions to this Contract must be in writing.<br /><br />
 (vi) This Contract does not create any sort of agency, partnership, joint venture, or<br /><br />
      other business entity.<br /><br />
(vii) Time is of the essence. This means that if this Contract goes before a<br /><br />
      court, the court should honor the time limits and deadlines in this Contract as<br /><br />
      firm unless the parties agree to waive them.<br /><br />
(viii)If a court determines that some part of this Contract is invalid, the parties<br /><br />
      want the court to strike only the offending provision and not the entire<br /><br />
      Contract.<br /><br />

      Contract. If a court is ever asked to admit the text of this Contract into<br /><br />

      determining the rights and obligations of the parties.<br /><br />

B8.42 - Liability for Loss<br /><br />
<br /><br />
If standing trees or TIMBER are destroyed or damaged by an unexpected event that significantly changes its nature, such as fire, wind, flood, insects, disease, or similar cause, the party holding title shall bear the value loss resulting from each destruction or damage. This Section shall not be construed to relieve either party of liability for negligence. <br /><br />

B8.5 - Force Majeure<br /><br />
<br /><br />
(a) The term force majeure, as used in this Contract, means any cause beyond the reasonable control of the parties and which the parties could not foresee and/or reasonably provide against and that prevents the parties from wholly or partially performing any respective duties under this Contract for 15 consecutive days or more (except as noted). Force majeure includes the following:<br /><br />
(i) Acts of God, accidents, fires, explosions, earthquake, flood, violent storm, hurricane, lighting, or other natural disasters;<br /><br />
(ii) War (whether declared or not), revolution, insurrection, invasions, acts of public enemies, or hostilities;<br /><br />
(iii) Riot, civil commotion, sabotage, strikes and similar labor related disputes (if continuing for a period of 60 days or more), or civil uprising (not resulting from a negligent act of the employer);<br /><br />
(iv) Epidemic;<br /><br />
(v) Expropriation of facilities or goods;<br /><br />
(vi) Unforeseen restrictions on trade, embargoes, blockades, or other activities imposed by any sovereign; or<br /><br />
(vii) AUTHORITY demands by written order that operations be delayed or interrupted for reasons other than suspension for breach of the Contract. <br /><br />
(b) The rainy season is foreseeable and, therefore, does not qualify for force majeure. <br /><br />
(c) Failure on the part of HOLDER or of the GOVERNMENT to fulfill any of the terms and conditions of this Contract, other than HOLDER's obligations to make payments of money that accrued before the commencement of this force majeure, shall not be deemed to be a breach of the Contract by either party, insofar as such failure arose by force majeure. <br /><br />
(d) If through force majeure, the fulfillment by HOLDER of the terms and conditions of this Contract is delayed, the period of such delay shall be added to the periods fixed by this Contract. <br /><br />
(e) The party failing to fulfill the terms and conditions of this Contract because of force majeure shall give written notice to the other party of the obligations affected and the reasons for failure within 30 days after the occurrence. <br /><br />
(f) Any party who fails because of force majeure to perform its obligations shall, upon the cessation of the force majeure, take all reasonable steps within its power to make good and resume, with the least possible delay, compliance with those obligations. <br /><br />
