4.0    Project Area Description :                                            <br /><br /><br />
4.1    Terrain/Physiography -                                                <br /><br /><br />
The prospect area is characterized by moderate to rolling                    <br /><br /><br />
topography with conspicuous domal/conical promontories, flanked b\           <br /><br /><br />
relatively flat terrain. Rugged topography with sharp ridges is noted        <br /><br /><br />
in the prospect area.                                                        <br /><br /><br />
4.2    Accessibility-                                                        <br /><br /><br />
From Masbate, the prospect site is easily accessible to any type             <br /><br /><br />
of motorized vehicle utilizing the Masbate-Milagros road or the              <br /><br /><br />
Aroroy- Baleno road. On the other hand, flights from Manila to               <br /><br /><br />
Masbate and vice versa is available on a daily regular basis with an         <br /><br /><br />
air travel time of one (1) hour and twenty (20) minutes.                     <br /><br /><br />
4.3    -' Drainage System/s -                                                <br /><br /><br />
The prospect area is bounded by the Guinobatan River on the                  <br /><br /><br />
east and northeast portion and by the tributaries of Lanang River at         <br /><br /><br />
the southwest. Both rivers drain westward to the Port Barrera Cove.          <br /><br /><br />
4.4    Vegetation -                                                          <br /><br /><br />
The area has limited forest trees. It is generally covered ssitli            <br /><br /><br />
cogon grasses and shrubs suited for grazing. Mangroves and swamp)            <br /><br /><br />
areas are found in the eastern portion of the prospect along the             <br /><br /><br />
coastal areas. The hills and valleys are practically barren except for       <br /><br /><br />
corn farmlands, wild bananas, coconuts and vegetative crops which            <br /><br /><br />
grow in clusters.                                                            <br /><br /><br />
4.5    Land Use -                                                            <br /><br /><br />
The proposed area is classified as mineral lands. The                        <br /><br /><br />
surrounding areas are suited for grazing hence, the presence of many         <br /><br /><br />
cattle ranches. Although an estimated 1/10 of its total land area is Hat     <br /><br /><br />
and suitable for agricultural/vegetative crops, the residents prefer the     <br /><br /><br />
quick and more lucrative gold panning as their means of livelihood.          <br /><br /><br />

                                                                                 <br /><br /><br />
 The exploration areas are generally characterized by                            <br /><br /><br />

 comprising barangays.                                                           <br /><br /><br />

 4.1.2    Land Use/Capability                                               <br /><br /><br />
                                                                            <br /><br /><br />
 The prospect areas are classified as mineral lands. The                    <br /><br /><br />
 surrounding areas are suited for grazing hence the                         <br /><br /><br />
 presence of many cattle ranches. Although an estimated                     <br /><br /><br />
 1/10 of its total land area is flat and suitable for                       <br /><br /><br />
 agricultural/vegetative crops, the residents prefer the                    <br /><br /><br />

 materials are found along areas where slopes are more                      <br /><br /><br />
 favorable and closer to the access road.                                   <br /><br /><br />

 The topographical relief features distinct vegetation                      <br /><br /><br />
 growth, reflective of the dominant soil type found in the                  <br /><br /><br />
 applied area. The mountainous eastern and southern                         <br /><br /><br />
 sections of the prospect area have shallow soil                            <br /><br /><br />
 accumulations (clays and talus), mainly due to moderate                    <br /><br /><br />
 and steeply sloping ground.                                                <br /><br /><br />
 The eastern mountain areas generally represent the                         <br /><br /><br />
 mineralized ground where most of the exploration                           <br /><br /><br />
 activities will be conducted. Poor vegetation growth at                    <br /><br /><br />
 this section is largely due to thin and mineralized soil                   <br /><br /><br />
 cover. Slight to moderate erosion are confined on rill                     <br /><br /><br />
 and gullies.                                                               <br /><br /><br />
 The unmineralized western parts of the applied area are                    <br /><br /><br />
 characterized by flat to very gently sloping grounds,                      <br /><br /><br />
 mostly grasslands and patchy riceflelds in alluvium soil.                  <br /><br /><br />

4.2.2 Hydrology                                                           <br /><br /><br />
During rainy season, run-off water in the area is high due                <br /><br /><br />
to frequent stormy weather and prolonged monsoon rains                    <br /><br /><br />
aggravated by the rugged topography. Rare instances of                    <br /><br /><br />
river swelling and flooding occur, normally at the height                 <br /><br /><br />
of strong typhoons. The Lanang River is the main source                   <br /><br /><br />
of both domestic and industrial water of the local                        <br /><br /><br />
residents and the mining community all year round.                        <br /><br /><br />
From historic record, the annual average water inflow                     <br /><br /><br />
during the rainy season far exceeds 6,000 gpm, while dry                  <br /><br /><br />
season average inflow is about 1,000 gpm. Excellent                       <br /><br /><br />
climate and uninhabited watershed areas contribute to                     <br /><br /><br />
continued water supply to the inhabitants. The prospect                   <br /><br /><br />
areas, however, are outside the watershed.                                <br /><br /><br />
4.3    Climatology/Meteorology                                            <br /><br /><br />
The climate is relatively humid with annual rainfall varying              <br /><br /><br />
from 24 to 26 cms. The average rainfall data were obtained                <br /><br /><br />
from the Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and                          <br /><br /><br />
Astronomical Services Administration (PAG-ASA), Masbate                   <br /><br /><br />
Station. Rainy season is from June to January with dry season             <br /><br /><br />
for the rest of the months. The area has an excellent climate             <br /><br /><br />
and good air circulation due to the absence of heavy vehicular            <br /><br /><br />
traffic, high-rise buildings and other industries.                        <br /><br /><br />
The temperature is warm during the months of March, April                 <br /><br /><br />
and May. From June to February, the climate is generally cool             <br /><br /><br />
with coolest period during December to February.                          <br /><br /><br />

                   4.4.2    Structural Setting                                <br /><br /><br />
                                                                              <br /><br /><br />
                   The locations of all of the five (5) gold mining           <br /><br /><br />
                   districts are along the so-called Philippine Rift zone or  <br /><br /><br />
                   the Philippine Mobile Belt. This tectonic belt has some    <br /><br /><br />
                   genetical control on the gold deposits in the country.     <br /><br /><br />
                   The Masbate vein system is thought to be a northwest       <br /><br /><br />
                   split or branch of the rift zone, which controlled the     <br /><br /><br />
                   mega fractures and the alignment of the volcanic plugs.    <br /><br /><br />
                   These splits and branches of the rift zone could have      <br /><br /><br />
                   potential for gold mineralization.                         <br /><br /><br />
                                                                              <br /><br /><br />
                   The prospect area is transected by several                 <br /><br /><br />
                   northwest trending tension fractures and is apparently     <br /><br /><br />
                   situated on an elevated fault block reflecting the great   <br /><br /><br />
                   upward push by extensive and continuous volcanic           <br /><br /><br />
                   activity. The northwest recurrent-shear zones are          <br /><br /><br />
                   further complicated by the post -tension fractures. An     <br /><br /><br />
                   extensive exploration-drilling program must confirm        <br /><br /><br />
                   these.                                                     <br /><br /><br />

4.5    Biological Environment                                       <br /><br /><br />
4.5.1    Terrestrial plants and animals                             <br /><br /><br />
i.    Animals                                                       <br /><br /><br />
Cattle ranches are abundant in the surrounding                      <br /><br /><br />
areas. Herds of cows are seen outside the prospect                  <br /><br /><br />
areas grazing for green vegetation. Though some                     <br /><br /><br />
of these ranches are inside other MPSA                              <br /><br /><br />
application, these are outside and distant from the                 <br /><br /><br />
proposed exploration projects under this                            <br /><br /><br />
application (APSA-V-0031).                                          <br /><br /><br />
Domesticated animals such as carabaos, horses,                      <br /><br /><br />
cows, goats, pigs and chicken are also found in the                 <br /><br /><br />
area. Their number is limited since the site is not                 <br /><br /><br />
an agricultural land and very few residents are                     <br /><br /><br />
engage in farming. A few specie of migrating birds                  <br /><br /><br />
are seen in the areas but is not home to any form                   <br /><br /><br />
of exotic or rare animal species that requires                      <br /><br /><br />
protection. The ecological niche occupied by the                    <br /><br /><br />
native fauna is almost taken over by the                            <br /><br /><br />
introduced animal species. Wildlife, such as deer,                  <br /><br /><br />
wild boar, monitor lizard and monkeys is no longer                  <br /><br /><br />
in the area.                                                        <br /><br /><br />
ii.    Plants                                                       <br /><br /><br />
The area is generally covered with cogon grasses                    <br /><br /><br />
and shrubs suited for grazing. Mangroves and                        <br /><br /><br />
swampy areas are found in the northwestern side                     <br /><br /><br />
of the prospected mining claims and along the                       <br /><br /><br />
coastal areas of Port Barrera. Occasional trees can                 <br /><br /><br />
be seen along the banks of creeks, brooks, streams                  <br /><br /><br />
and rivers. The hills and valleys are practically                   <br /><br /><br />
barren except for patches of rice and corn                          <br /><br /><br />
farmlands, vegetative crops, and coconut trees,                     <br /><br /><br />
which grow in clusters.                                             <br /><br /><br />
4.5.2    Marine Plants and Animals                                  <br /><br /><br />
A number of fish species such as carp, bangus and tilapia           <br /><br /><br />
can be found in the Lanang and Guinobatan Rivers.                   <br /><br /><br />
4.6    Socio-Economic Environment                                   <br /><br /><br />
The development of the Municipality of Aroroy lags behind in        <br /><br /><br />
comparison to the booming and buzzling urban centers in the         <br /><br /><br />
neighboring provinces. Except for the few elite residents, the      <br /><br /><br />
economic and social conditions of the majority are below            <br /><br /><br />
standard. About 10% of the town folks depend on gold panning        <br /><br /><br />
as their main source of livelihood. Other residents are engaged     <br /><br /><br />
in fishing, small-scale business like buying and selling, eateries, <br /><br /><br />
sari-sari stores and the transportation business utilizing          <br /><br /><br />
jeepneys and motorized bancas. Some are employed in the             <br /><br /><br />
government offices.                                                 <br /><br /><br />

6.0 IDENTIFICATION OF POTENTIAL ENVIRONMENTAL EFFECTS                  <br /><br /><br />
6.1    On Land                                                         <br /><br /><br />
With the road network already existing during the operation of         <br /><br /><br />
Atlas Consolidated Mining and Development Corporation                  <br /><br /><br />
(ACMDC), there is no significant potential environmental effects       <br /><br /><br />
identified on constructing access roads. The planned                   <br /><br /><br />
exploration activities will only involve the construction of short     <br /><br /><br />
and narrow feeder roads.                                               <br /><br /><br />
The possible environmental effect may come during movement             <br /><br /><br />
of drilling equipment and drill site preparations. The drilling        <br /><br /><br />
operations will strip rock materials and the vegetation growth         <br /><br /><br />
within the confines of the drilling site. Drilling operations will     <br /><br /><br />
produce some noise and fumes/dust. There will be minimal               <br /><br /><br />
surface disturbance and soil contamination considering that            <br /><br /><br />
existing roads and short extensions will be utilized for access to     <br /><br /><br />
the drill sites.                                                       <br /><br /><br />
The exploration campsites will be properly maintained with             <br /><br /><br />
proper sanitation and hygiene. Non-biodegradable waste (e.g.           <br /><br /><br />
container drums, lube oils, grease and other exploration waste         <br /><br /><br />
will be removed from the exploration site and disposed of              <br /><br /><br />
properly. The exploration team will be quartered at the existing       <br /><br /><br />
mine facilities since the housing compound is close to the             <br /><br /><br />
exploration sites.                                                     <br /><br /><br />
6.2    Changes in Hydrology                                            <br /><br /><br />
Major flooding will be a remote possibility due to a limited           <br /><br /><br />
watershed and drainage network. There will be no major                 <br /><br /><br />
ecological alteration. Vegetation that would be destroyed will         <br /><br /><br />
be replenished or compensated by the present                           <br /><br /><br />
reforestation/afforestation project of the company. Existing           <br /><br /><br />
hydrologic cycle will thus be maintained. Water used in drilling       <br /><br /><br />
