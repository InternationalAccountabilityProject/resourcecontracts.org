 CANATUAN GOLD PROJECT                                                  <br /><br /><br />
 Siocon, Zamboanga del Norte                                            <br /><br /><br />
               PROPOSED TWO-YEAR EXPLORATION WORK PROGRAM               <br /><br /><br />
                                                                        <br /><br /><br />
                                                                        <br /><br /><br />
NAME AND ADDRESS OF COMPANY/PROPONENT:                                  <br /><br /><br />
*    Benguet Corporation with postal address at #12 San Miguel          <br /><br /><br />
Avenue, Mandaluyong City, Metro Manila as OPERATOR for                  <br /><br /><br />
and on behalf of Ramon V. Bosque, Claimowner.                           <br /><br /><br />
LOCATION OF PROJECT:                                                    <br /><br /><br />
*    The Canatuan Gold Project is located close to the western          <br /><br /><br />
coast of Mindanao more specifically situated in Sitio                   <br /><br /><br />
Canatuan. Barangay Tabayo, Municipality of Siocon,                      <br /><br /><br />
Province of Zamboanga del Norte, Philippines. It is bounded             <br /><br /><br />
by Latitudes 7° 43' 30" to 7° 44’ 30" N and Longitude 122° 16’          <br /><br /><br />
00" to 122° 18’ 00” E, more or less.                                    <br /><br /><br />
AREA OR S ZE OF COVERAGE:                                               <br /><br /><br />
*    The Project is composed of six (6) contiguous meridional block     <br /><br /><br />
applied for MPSA by Ramon V. Bosque with a total land area              <br /><br /><br />
of 508.3396 hectares as per actual transit survey.                      <br /><br /><br />
DESCRIPTION OF PROJECT AREA:                                            <br /><br /><br />
*    The opography of the project area is characterized by              <br /><br /><br />
steep-crested hills and deeply incised valleys. It is accessible        <br /><br /><br />
from Zamboanga City to R.T. Lim which is about 120 road                 <br /><br /><br />
kilomoteres via Highway 14, thence through 45 km long old               <br /><br /><br />
logging road leading to the project site. The tributaries form          <br /><br /><br />
dendritic pattern which largely flows south to southeasterly to         <br /><br /><br />
the main Litoban River which flows westerly to Siocon Bay.              <br /><br /><br />
The area was previously logged-over both by commercial                  <br /><br /><br />
logging and local residents. In addition to sparse second               <br /><br /><br />
         . growth trees, patches of kaingin farms with upland rice,     <br /><br /><br />
﻿rootcrops arid bananas cover the rolling terrains and flat        <br /><br /><br />
areas.                                                            <br /><br /><br />
DESCRIPTION OF EXPLORATION PROGRAM:                               <br /><br /><br />
A.    Researan Work                                               <br /><br /><br />
*    Between May and September 1991, Benguet Corporation          <br /><br /><br />
conducted preliminary field verification, followed by             <br /><br /><br />
mapping and sampling of some of the excavations of small          <br /><br /><br />
scale miners. Results indicated favorable gold mineralization     <br /><br /><br />
which suggested further systematic geological investigation.      <br /><br /><br />
B.    Reconnaissance/Regional Survey/Studies                      <br /><br /><br />
*    The initial information gathered from the excavations done   <br /><br /><br />
by the small scale miners will be a good background. As           <br /><br /><br />
such, reconnaissance or regional survey/studies will not be       <br /><br /><br />
necessary. Thus, fieldwork will be initiated immediately with     <br /><br /><br />
semi-detailed survey or follow-up studies then subsequently       <br /><br /><br />
followed by detailed survey or studies.                           <br /><br /><br />
C.    Semi-detailed Survey/Follow-Up Studies                      <br /><br /><br />
*    Semi-detailed mapping/alteration studies will be conducted   <br /><br /><br />
over the entire 508.3369 hectares for a period of three (3)       <br /><br /><br />
months covering the tributaries, road cuts, trails, stream        <br /><br /><br />
sediment, pan- con and rock sampling involving 18 men and         <br /><br /><br />
staff with an estimated cost of PI98,150.00. The information      <br /><br /><br />
goithered will be consolidated into a 1:10.000 scale map          <br /><br /><br />
showing lithology, structural controls, alterations and           <br /><br /><br />
mineralization which will be utilized to plan targets for         <br /><br /><br />
detailed geological mapping and sub-surface programs.             <br /><br /><br />
*    Geophysical Survey will be undertaken by utilizing the Time  <br /><br /><br />
Domain Electro-Magnetic (TDEM) and Magnetometer                   <br /><br /><br />
applications along 4000 lineal meters. This activity will be      <br /><br /><br />
contracted by McPhar/other Laboratories with a budget of          <br /><br /><br />
P500,000.00. The resulting data will produce maps/graphs          <br /><br /><br />
showing the conductivity, resistivity, magnetic susceptibility of <br /><br /><br />
the available metals and/or elements, contour and profile         <br /><br /><br />
﻿maps, which will be used in planning programs for sub¬                <br /><br /><br />
surface investigations.                                               <br /><br /><br />
*    Sub-surface Investigation will initially involve the mapping and <br /><br /><br />
sampling of shafts and adits excavated by the small scale             <br /><br /><br />
miners numbering to about 50 openings with about 300                  <br /><br /><br />
meters aggregate length/depth. Approximate channel                    <br /><br /><br />
samples to be collected will be about 600 bags which will be          <br /><br /><br />
assayed for Gold by fire assay and Ag, Cu, Pb, Zn, As by AAS          <br /><br /><br />
method. This undertaking will be done by the same                     <br /><br /><br />
manpower (9 men) as earlier established in Geological                 <br /><br /><br />
Mapping for an approximate duration of three (3) months               <br /><br /><br />
with a cost of about P198,150.00.                                     <br /><br /><br />
D. Topographic Survey                                                 <br /><br /><br />
*    Topographic Survey will be initially undertaken over an area     <br /><br /><br />
of 1.5 km by 1.0 km or 150 hectares and establish a base map          <br /><br /><br />
of scale 1:2000 with 5 meter contour intervals. This activity will    <br /><br /><br />
involve 6 men with an estimated budget of P230,000.00 for a           <br /><br /><br />
seven (7) month period.                                               <br /><br /><br />
DETAILED SURVEY STUDIES                                               <br /><br /><br />
*    Detailed Geological Mapping will be conducted with               <br /><br /><br />
Compass-tape traverses over the identified “economic zone"            <br /><br /><br />
area encompassing the tributaries, road cuts/access trails,           <br /><br /><br />
with emphasis on the structural controls, lithology, alterations      <br /><br /><br />
and mineralization utilizing 1:500 scale field maps and               <br /><br /><br />
transposed into 1:2000 base maps. Based on the small scale            <br /><br /><br />
miners operations the "economic" zone is about 150 hectares           <br /><br /><br />
wl ere activities will last for about 12 months supported by 9        <br /><br /><br />
men with an estimated budget of P792,600.00. Maps to be               <br /><br /><br />
produced will show lithology, alterations mineralized and ore         <br /><br /><br />
zones.                                                                <br /><br /><br />
*    Derailed Geochemical survey will be applied over an              <br /><br /><br />
approximate area of 1.5 km X 1.0 km or 150 hectares at 50 m           <br /><br /><br />
X 100 m grid where some 300 soil samples will be collected.           <br /><br /><br />
Gold and silver assays will be by fire assay and Cu, Pb, Zn, As,      <br /><br /><br />
Sb and Hg will be analyzed by AAS. This activity will be              <br /><br /><br />
complemented by 5 men over a period of two (2) months                 <br /><br /><br />
﻿ with an estimated cost of P75,000.00. The results will be                          <br /><br /><br />
 translated into 1:2000 scale maps showing anomalous zones                          <br /><br /><br />
 and isograd maps per element.                                                      <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                    <br /><br /><br />
                                                                                    <br /><br /><br />
 Sub-surface investigation will utilize diamond drilling (coring)                   <br /><br /><br />
 initially with 18 holes with a total length of 750 meters and                      <br /><br /><br />
 completed in two (2) months period. Some 150 sludge                                <br /><br /><br />
 samples and about 100 core samples is expected to be                               <br /><br /><br />
 collected. Estimated cost is about PI,875,000.00. In addition,                     <br /><br /><br />
 about 50 test pits has been programmed to test certain                             <br /><br /><br />
 anomalous zones with an aggregate depth of 300 meters                              <br /><br /><br />
 which is projected to be completed in about 3.5 months with                        <br /><br /><br />
 an estimated cost of PI40,000.00                                                   <br /><br /><br />
