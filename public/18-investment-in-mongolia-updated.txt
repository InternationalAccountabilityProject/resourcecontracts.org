<br /><br />
In accordance with Article 29 of the Minerals Law of Mongolia (hereinafter referred to as the<br /><br />
“Minerals Law”), Resolution Number 40 dated 4 December 2008 and Resolution Number 57<br /><br />
dated 16 July 2009 respectively of the State Great Khural and Government Resolution Number<br /><br />
308 dated 6 October 2009:<br /><br />
The Minister of Finance, the Minister of Mineral Resources and Energy and the Minister<br /><br />
of Nature, Environment and Tourism, together representing and upon authorization of the<br /><br />
Government of Mongolia;<br /><br />
and<br /><br />
Ivanhoe Mines Mongolia Inc LLC (hereinafter referred to as the “Investor”);<br /><br />
and<br /><br />
Ivanhoe Mines Ltd (hereinafter referred to as “Ivanhoe Mines Ltd”);<br /><br />
and<br /><br />
Rio Tinto International Holdings Limited (hereinafter referred to as “Rio Tinto”);<br /><br />
have entered into this Agreement on this 6th day of October 2009;<br /><br />
Having regard to the following:<br /><br />
The Investor having invested a significant amount of capital in the OT Project through<br /><br />
the financial capability of Ivanhoe Mines Ltd and Rio Tinto;<br /><br />
Article 4.1.12 of the Minerals Law and Resolution Number 27 of the State Great Khural<br /><br />
dated 6 February 2007;<br /><br />
The Private Placement Agreement dated 18 October 2006 between Ivanhoe Mines Ltd<br /><br />
and Rio Tinto (as amended);<br /><br />
The purpose of this Agreement being to define and regulate a relationship in regard to<br /><br />
maintaining for a certain period a stable Tax and operational environment, the sale of<br /><br />
Products at international market prices by the Investor, the guarantee of the Investor’s<br /><br />
right to use and spend its income at its own discretion, the amount and term of the<br /><br />
investment, the undertaking of mining activities with minimum damage to the<br /><br />
environment and human health, the rehabilitation of the environment, not to have a<br /><br />
negative effect on other industries and operations, the social and economic development<br /><br />
of the Southern Gobi region and creation of new jobs, the creation of business<br /><br />
opportunities for Mongolian companies and individuals, compensation for damages to<br /><br />
property, basis for the termination of this Agreement, and rights and obligations of the<br /><br />
<br /><br />

Resolution Number 57 of the State Great Khural dated 16 July 2009 has resolved to authorise the<br /><br />

1.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
CHAPTER ONE: GENERAL<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.1.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Except as provided by Clause 15.26, this Agreement shall come into effect on and from<br /><br />
the Effective Date and remain in effect, in accordance with Article 29.3 of the Minerals<br /><br />
Law, for an initial period of 30 (thirty) years.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.2.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The Investor shall have a right to apply for and obtain from competent authorities in<br /><br />
accordance with relevant laws and regulations of Mongolia all leases, licenses, permits,<br /><br />
work visas, customs clearance, easements and rights of way (permits for land to be used<br /><br />
for road corridors and road facilities), approvals of competent authorities and other<br /><br />
similar consents required in connection with the OT Project ("Permits") and the<br /><br />
Government shall provide support requested by the Investor in this respect and shall<br /><br />
resolve the Investor’s applications to obtain such Permits in accordance with relevant<br /><br />
laws and regulations of Mongolia.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.3.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Except as provided by Clause 2.24.2, Taxes payable by the Investor shall remain<br /><br />
Stabilized (as described in Clause 2.1 and Clause 2.24).<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.4.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The Investor is hereby granted the rights to market, sell and export its Products at<br /><br />
international market prices and to freely expend and repatriate its sale proceeds in<br /><br />
Mongolian togrogs and foreign currencies.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.5.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
This Agreement applies to the whole range of the Investor's activities in connection with<br /><br />
the OT Project that includes prospecting and exploring within the Contract Area,<br /><br />
separating and mining minerals from land surface and subsoil, construction and operation<br /><br />
of infrastructure for transportation, power, water and other infrastructure facilities,<br /><br />
processing (including crushing, grinding, floating and filtering), producing Products,<br /><br />
marketing, mine closure, rehabilitation, ore stockpiling, waste and tailings management<br /><br />
and all other connected activities (hereinafter referred to as the "Core Operations").<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.6.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The State shall own 34% (thirty four percent) of the common shares in the Investor, and,<br /><br />
within 1 (one) year after this Agreement is extended in accordance with Clause 15.11,<br /><br />
have the option to own a further 16% (sixteen percent) of the common shares in the<br /><br />
Investor, on terms mutually agreed as contained in the Shareholders' Agreement. The<br /><br />
State warrants that this Clause 1.6 satisfies all obligations under laws or resolutions of the<br /><br />
State Great Khural to issue or transfer shares in the Investor to the Government.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.7.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The Investor shall use its best endeavours in the course of its operations to promote<br /><br />
development of the Southern Gobi region, including the creation of employment<br /><br />
opportunities.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.8.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The State agrees that the signing of this Agreement and the Shareholders' Agreement, and<br /><br />
the issue of common shares in the Investor equal to 34% (thirty four percent) of the total<br /><br />
common share capital of the Investor to the State pursuant to the Shareholders’<br /><br />

<br /><br />
Agreement, satisfies all of the obligations of the Investor under Article 5.5 of the<br /><br />

1.9.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The obligations under Article 5.6 of the Minerals Law are to be fulfilled under clause<br /><br />
15.3(c) of the Shareholders’ Agreement, and the Government shall be liable for, and<br /><br />
assume the obligations for, any failure of SHC to meet the requirements of clause 15.3(c)<br /><br />
of the Shareholders’ Agreement.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.10.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
Any relationship which is not governed by this Agreement shall be regulated by the<br /><br />
effective laws and regulations of Mongolia, and the international treaties to which<br /><br />
Mongolia is a party.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.11.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The Investor has the right to conduct the operations of mining and processing minerals<br /><br />
from the underground mine area and the open pit mine area, and producing Products,<br /><br />
within mining license 6709A of the Oyu Tolgoi Deposit. The reserves for these areas are<br /><br />
registered in the national registry of reserves, and the Feasibility Study for these areas has<br /><br />
been submitted to the State administrative authority in charge of geology and mining for<br /><br />
its consideration in accordance with existing laws and regulations. Consideration of the<br /><br />
Feasibility Study will be concluded in 150 (one hundred and fifty) days from the date of<br /><br />
submission.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
1.12.<br /><br />
<br /><br />
The terms used in this Agreement shall be construed in accordance with Chapter 16<br /><br />
(Sixteen).<br /><br />
